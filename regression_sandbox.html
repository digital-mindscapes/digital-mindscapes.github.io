Statistical Methodology

The Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression estimates the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent predictors by minimizing the sum of the squares of the differences between the observed and predicted values.

Y = β0 + β1X1 + β2X2 + ... + βkXk + ε

Model Evaluation

  • R-Squared: The proportion of the variance in the dependent variable that is predictable from the independent variable(s).
  • p-value (Pr(>|t|)): The probability of finding the observed, or more extreme, results when the null hypothesis (that the coefficient is 0) is true.
  • Coefficients: The estimated change in the dependent variable for every unit increase in the predictor, holding other variables constant.

Technical Notes

  • Computations are handled entirely on the client-side via the Math.js matrix algebra and jStat probability libraries.
  • Standard errors and p-values are derived from the variance-covariance matrix (XTX)-1 σ2.
  • Missing or null records for any selected variable drop that record entirely (Listwise Deletion).
